Local Business Websites

Websites built around how local customers browse, decide, and take action.

A set of retail and restaurant websites focused on clear information, mobile usability, customer actions, and practical launch workflows.

Problem

Local businesses often need websites that do a few important jobs extremely clearly: explain what the business offers, make hours and location easy to find, support mobile visitors, and move customers toward calling, ordering, visiting, or getting directions.

Solution approach

Each site was designed around the customer's actual decision path. The work combined content structure, image selection, mobile layout, navigation, hosting, launch setup, and the small details that make a local site useful day to day.

Sites included

  • Tran's Market: a retail site for products, store information, directions, and contact options.
  • Arundel House of Pizza: a restaurant site for menu browsing, calls, directions, reviews, and online ordering.
  • The Jewel of India: a restaurant site for menu browsing, catering information, and two-location discovery.

Operational value

The value is not just a nicer website. The sites give customers fewer reasons to call with basic questions, make the right actions easier on mobile, and give each business a clearer public presence that can be maintained after launch.
