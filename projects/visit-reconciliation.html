Visit Reconciliation Case Study

Reconciling multi-state operational data at the territory and client level.

A reporting and reconciliation workflow designed to turn raw visit activity into weekly rollups, state-specific views, and exception-based review.

Problem

A multi-state home healthcare operation needed clearer weekly visibility into visit activity by territory and client. Raw exports made it difficult to see exceptions quickly, compare activity consistently, or catch discrepancies before they became larger operational or compliance issues.

Solution approach

The workbook was designed around repeatable comparison logic. Instead of forcing a full manual audit every week, the workflow organized raw visit data into state-specific views and highlighted exceptions that needed attention.

Workflow improvements

  • Created weekly rollups by territory and by client from raw visit data.
  • Built state-specific reconciliation views for multi-state operations.
  • Focused review time on exceptions instead of every record.
  • Made recurring reporting easier to refresh and explain.
  • Improved visibility into discrepancies before they became downstream problems.

Operational value

The value was a clearer review process: fewer manual passes through raw data, faster visibility into what needed follow-up, and a repeatable structure staff could use week after week.
